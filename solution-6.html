Problem 6 - Solving an Equation

To solve the equation 2x + 5 = 13, we need to isolate x on one side of the equation.

Let's start by subtracting 5 from both sides:

2x + 5 - 5 = 13 - 5

2x = 8

Next, divide both sides by 2 to solve for x:

x = 8/2

Simplifying the fraction gives:

x = 4

Answer: The value of x is 4.
