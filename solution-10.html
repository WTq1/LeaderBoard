Problem 10 - Maximum Height

To find the maximum height reached by the ball, we need to consider its vertical motion. The key equation to use is:

Final Velocity² = Initial Velocity² + 2 * Acceleration * Distance

Since the ball is thrown vertically upward, the acceleration due to gravity acts in the opposite direction. Therefore, we take acceleration as -9.8 m/s² (negative sign for downward acceleration).

Given that the initial velocity is 20 m/s, we can substitute these values into the equation:

0² = 20² + 2 * (-9.8) * Distance

Simplifying the equation:

-400 = -19.6 * Distance

Dividing both sides of the equation by -19.6:

Distance = 400 / 19.6

Distance ≈ 20.41 meters

Answer: The ball will reach a maximum height of approximately 20.41 meters.
