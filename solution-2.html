Problem 2 - Filling a Tank

To find the time it takes to fill the cylindrical tank completely, we need to calculate the volume of the tank and divide it by the rate at which water is being pumped.

The formula for the volume of a cylinder is:

V = πr²h

Given that the diameter of the tank is 10m, the radius (r) is 5m.

Let's assume the height of the tank is h meters.

The volume of the tank can be calculated as:

V = 3.14159 * 5² * h

V = 78.5398h

If the water is being pumped into the tank at a rate of 2m³ per minute, we can set up the equation:

78.5398h / 2 = t

Simplifying the equation:

39.2699h = t

Answer: It will take approximately 39.2699h minutes to fill the cylindrical tank completely, where "h" represents the height of the tank.
