Problem 11 - Acceleration on an Inclined Plane

To find the acceleration of the block sliding down the inclined plane, we can consider the forces acting on the block. The key equation to use is:

Net Force = (Mass * Gravity * sinθ) - (Frictional Force)

Given that the mass of the block is 2 kg, the angle of the inclined plane is 30°, and the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.2, we can substitute these values into the equation:

Net Force = (2 * 9.8 * sin30°) - (0.2 * 2 * 9.8 * cos30°)

Simplifying the equation:

Net Force = (2 * 4.9 * 0.5) - (0.2 * 2 * 9.8 * 0.866)

Net Force = 4.9 - 3.3824

Net Force = 1.5176 N

Using Newton's second law of motion, we can relate the net force to the acceleration:

Net Force = Mass * Acceleration

1.5176 = 2 * Acceleration

Acceleration = 1.5176 / 2

Acceleration ≈ 0.7588 m/s²

Answer: The acceleration of the block as it slides down the inclined plane is approximately 0.7588 m/s².
