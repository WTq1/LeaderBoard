Problem 8 - Equivalent Resistance

To find the equivalent resistance of the two resistors connected in parallel (R1 and R2), we can use the following formula:

1/Req = 1/R1 + 1/R2

Where:
Req = equivalent resistance
R1 = resistance of the first resistor
R2 = resistance of the second resistor

Given that R1 = 10 ohms and R2 = 15 ohms, we can substitute these values into the formula:

1/Req = 1/10 + 1/15

Combining the fractions:

1/Req = (3 + 2) / 30

1/Req = 5 / 30

1/Req = 1 / 6

Therefore, Req = 6 ohms

Answer: The equivalent resistance of the combination is 6 ohms.
