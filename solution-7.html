Problem 7 - Diver's Jump

To find out how long it takes for the diver to reach the highest point of the jump, we can use the concept of projectile motion.

Given that the initial velocity of the diver is 5m/s upward, we know that the acceleration due to gravity acts in the opposite direction, which is downward.

At the highest point of the jump, the diver's velocity becomes zero since the upward motion is decelerated by gravity.

We can use the following kinematic equation to find the time taken:

v = u + at

Where:
v = final velocity (0 m/s)
u = initial velocity (5 m/s)
a = acceleration due to gravity (-9.8 m/s^2)
t = time

Substituting the known values into the equation:

0 = 5 - 9.8t

9.8t = 5

t = 5 / 9.8
